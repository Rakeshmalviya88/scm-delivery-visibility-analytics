## Abstract
This project solves the delivery visibility challenge in supply chain operations, where low real-time transparency leads to delays and increased transportation cost. We designed a relational data model, generated realistic sample data, developed an interactive dashboard, and integrated machine learning to predict delay risk and shipment trends. The final solution helps managers monitor operational KPIs and take proactive action.

## 1. Problem Statement and Business Impact
Lack of visibility across delivery stages causes delayed decisions, late shipments, and unplanned transport expenses. The business impact includes SLA penalties, customer dissatisfaction, and reduced operational efficiency. Our solution addresses these gaps through route-level, carrier-level, and event-level monitoring.

## 2. Scope and SCM Area
### Scope
- Track shipment progression from dispatch to delivery.
- Compare carrier performance and route risk.
- Detect delay trends and cost overruns.
- Predict high-risk delays and short-term shipment demand.

### SCM Areas Addressed
- Transportation management.
- Order tracking and visibility.
- Exception management.
- Data-driven planning and logistics analytics.

## 3. Data Structure Design and Justification
### Database Design
The data model consists of six tables stored in MySQL:
- distribution_centers
- customers
- carriers
- routes
- shipments
- tracking_events

### Relationship Highlights
- One route links one origin DC and one destination customer.
- One shipment uses one route and one carrier.
- One shipment has multiple tracking events.

This structure captures both master and transactional data while supporting KPI computation and ML feature extraction.

## 4. Sample Data Preparation
We created realistic synthetic data for demonstration:
- 4 distribution centers
- 8 customer nodes
- 4 carriers
- 8 routes
- 900 shipments
- 4500 tracking events

The generated data includes operational factors such as weather score, traffic score, fuel index, planned/actual cost, and delivery delay.

## 5. Dashboard Design and Features
Dashboard technology: Python Dash + Plotly.

### KPI Cards
- On-time delivery percentage
- Average delay (minutes)
- Average cost overrun
- Predicted high-risk delay percentage

### Visualizations
- Weekly delay trend line chart
- Carrier performance bar chart
- Route risk scatter chart
- Monthly shipment demand with forecast

### Interactivity
- Month filter
- Carrier filter

The dashboard enables quick comparative analysis and exception-focused decisions.

## 6. Machine Learning Models and Benefits
### Models Applied
- RandomForestRegressor for delay minutes prediction.
- RandomForestClassifier for high-risk delay classification.

### Feature Set
- Distance, transit baseline, risk zone
- Weather, traffic, fuel index
- Shipment weight
- Carrier reliability and mode
- Shipment month and weekday

### Business Benefits
- Predict and prioritize risky deliveries before SLA breach.
- Allocate interventions where risk is highest.
- Improve carrier assignment and route planning.

## 7. Insights Derived
- High-risk zones and low-reliability combinations show larger delay spread.
- Some carriers have lower delay but higher cost; others are cheaper but less reliable.
- Seasonal trend shifts are visible in monthly shipment volume and delays.
- Delay cost significantly increases transportation overrun, supporting preventive action.

## 8. Challenges and Learnings
- Balancing realism and simplicity in synthetic data generation.
- Building explainable models suitable for management audience.
- Converting operational data into decision-oriented KPIs.

## 9. Future Improvements
- Integrate real-time GPS and IoT event streams.
- Add anomaly alerting and automated escalation.
- Use scenario simulation for route re-planning under disruption.
